The "Activity Ring"

The multicolored circle around a node visualizes the researcher's publication timeline.

  • Color = Time: Gradients move from Light (Oldest) to Dark (Newest).
  • Arc Length = Volume: Long arcs indicate years with many publications; short arcs indicate years with few.

Graph Anatomy:

  • Node Size: Proportional to total citations & works.
  • Center Color: Represents primary institution.
  • Connecting Lines: Thickness represents number of co-authored papers.

Metric Definitions

H-Index (Network) A researcher has H papers that have been cited at least H times within this specific dataset.
Betweenness "The Bridge." Identifies researchers who connect otherwise disconnected groups/institutions.
PageRank "The Influencer." Measures influence based on the quality and quantity of connections.
Brokerage "The Innovator." Ability to access non-redundant information from diverse clusters.

About the Constellation View

The Constellation view organizes publications based on thematic similarity rather than authorship.

  • Nodes: Represent individual publications. Size scales with citation count.
  • Links: Connect papers that share at least 2 major topics.
  • Colors: Indicate the dominant topic of the paper (see legend).
  • Clustering: Papers with similar topics naturally pull together, revealing thematic clusters in the research landscape.

The canvas detects cursor proximity for effortless exploration of dense clusters.
